How My Crochet Journey Began

My journey did not exactly start off great. I know what you’re thinking seeing my projects above this text, it seems like I am talented. Well, thank you, but that is hard work and a lot of starting from square one.

So, it first started when my mom all of a sudden started to crochet. The first time I saw it, I didn’t even know what it was, and I had no idea my mother knew such a handy creation. At first, I was not even interested. I just saw it and went about my day but then my sister became interested, and now she crochets as well.

I watched as my sister made a cool Spider-Man hat, that she later on gifted me. After I had seen that, I could no longer ignore it, which finally led me to asking how I could start. My mom started to explain it to me, and she taught it pretty well, but when I finally practiced it, I hated it, and I didn’t want anything to do with it.

My sister hated the fact that I gave up so easily since I was barely trying. She bashed me for it I didn’t pay any mind to her. The next day, they were still crocheting, but I refused to join them. Eventually, though, I started to pick up the hook and the yarn again because I just couldn’t get my sister’s words out of my head. My sister might be mean, but she is surely right.

After that, I worked on projects like a mini heart, the bags, a ruffled skirt even, and the Strawberry Shortcake hat even though that tutorial was in Spanish. A headband for me and one for a guy, which, in exchange, got me a ride to school for a week. I even got to sell one of my checkered bags for $40.

Also, when I was crocheting in school, people started to notice. And when I wore my crochet ruffled skirt to school, everybody wanted me to crochet something for them. Back then, I could have made a bunch of money, but unfortunately, I was moving to the Philippines, so I did not have time anymore.

Well, that is the end of my blog. Thanks for reading, and always remember don’t give up on something just because you suck at it or don’t like it at first, because you never know it might be your passion.
